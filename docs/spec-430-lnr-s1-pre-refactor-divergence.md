## Interpretation

The row-1 divergence is **alignment-driven, not behavioural-divergence**.

- HL starts its trace at master_clock 11 (drive8 in its reset sequence,
  about to write `$1803`). HL captures from cold reset through 60 s
  of activity.
- The legacy VICE baseline starts its `instructions` table at
  master_clock 12,359,824, which is ~12.5 s of master cycles into
  the boot. The legacy capture was set up for a fastloader debugging
  session, not for cold-boot canary use. It also captures `c64`-side
  instructions only at the first contract event — the drive-side
  contract instructions during cold-boot are absent from this baseline.

Consequence: a row-by-row diff against this legacy VICE baseline is
not informative beyond "DIVERGED at row 1, immediately." For the
Sprint 430 gate, that is sufficient to satisfy `expected=red`. It is
**not** sufficient as an IEC/VIA divergence oracle.
